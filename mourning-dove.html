About the Mourning Dove

The Mourning Dove is one of North America's most common and widespread birds. It has a slender body with a small head, long pointed tail, and soft gray-brown plumage. These birds are known for their graceful flight and distinctive mournful cooing that gives them their name.

Song Characteristics

The Mourning Dove's call is a soft, drawn-out cooing sound that is often described as sorrowful or melancholic. The typical pattern is a series of coos that sounds like "coo-OO-oo-oo-oo." As mentioned in the essay, their song is "almost nonexistent" compared to the more vocal robins and chickadees, making it a subtle component of Lullwater's soundscape.

At Lullwater Preserve

Mourning Doves can be found in Lullwater Preserve throughout the year, often perched on branches or walking along the ground searching for seeds. They are frequently spotted near the edges of wooded areas and around Candler Lake. While not as vocal as other birds at Lullwater, their gentle cooing adds a layer of peaceful ambiance to the preserve, especially during quieter moments.
