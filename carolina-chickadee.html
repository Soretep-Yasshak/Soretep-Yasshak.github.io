About the Carolina Chickadee

The Carolina Chickadee is a small, active bird found throughout the southeastern United States. It has a distinctive black cap and bib with white cheeks, gray wings, and a whitish belly. These social birds are known for their acrobatic feeding behavior and complex vocalizations.

Song Characteristics

The Carolina Chickadee has several distinct calls. Their most recognizable song is a four-note whistle that sounds like "fee-bee-fee-bay." They also have a characteristic "chickadee-dee-dee" call that gives them their name, with the number of "dee" notes often indicating the level of threat they perceive.

At Lullwater Preserve

Carolina Chickadees are year-round residents at Lullwater Preserve. They can often be spotted in the trees along the paths, flitting among branches and sometimes hanging upside down while foraging. Their cheerful calls contribute significantly to the soundscape of Lullwater throughout the year, particularly in the wooded areas.
